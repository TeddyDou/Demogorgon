Popular questions
I'm writing my thesis/dissertation. How can the Library help me?
Attend a GradProSkills workshop offered by a librarian or review the course materials
Use RefWorks to organize references and prepare bibliographies ( export/import instructions )
Consult the Copyright Guide for Thesis Preparation
Review previous theses from students your department in Spectrum , or worldwide in other research repositories
For materials you can't find at Concordia, use a BCI card to borrow from other Quebec libraries, suggest a purchase or submit a request via Interlibrary Loan
When you're ready to submit your thesis, review the instructions for how to prepare your thesis for deposit in Spectrum and how to deposit your thesis in Spectrum
Meet with your librarian for additional advice and help
How do I cite a ________________ in APA/MLA/Chicago style?
Start with our citation guides and if you're still unsure, Ask a Librarian .
I'm looking for information on ________________. Where should I start?
The best place to start is a Subject Guide created by a librarian with recommended resources such as databases, background sources, how-to guides, websites, and more. In some cases, there are even Course Guides with tips for your specific class.
Other places to look:
The Discovery Search to find articles, books, dictionaries, videos, sheet music, etc.
The Database Finder , to find a database in your subject area containing journal articles on a specific topic.
The Ask a Librarian service to chat with, email, or talk to a librarian for help.
