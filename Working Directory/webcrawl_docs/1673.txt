SUPPORT THROUGHOUT YOUR FIRST YEAR
Finding your rhythm as a new undergraduate or graduate student takes time. Help with the transition is available after orientation too!
Services for new students
Looking for tips on how to make your first year at Concordia a successful one?  Check out the events specifically tailored to new students that take place throughout the year.
  
Professional seminars
Let our Student Service Professionals help you transition successfully into your new university program.
First-Year Fridays
Graduate Experience seminars
Peer-to-peer interactions
Sometimes the best advice you can get is from a fellow student who’s been there too. Every term, Welcome Crew Mentors organize informal, interactive and fun events to pass on their knowledge about Concordia to students who are starting their academic journey. 
 
Check the schedule below.
Upcoming events
If no events are listed above, please check the events schedule again later.
Additional resources
Learning support
As you progress through classes, assignments and exams, you may find yourself in need of little academic help. Learning support exists in many forms at Concordia: one-on-one tutoring, study groups, workshops, language support and more.
Find learning support
Career planning and advice
Even though you’re just starting your program at Concordia, you may be thinking about your career goals or wondering where your degree will lead you, or even whether your degree is right for you.  Career and Planning Services (CAPS) can help you research careers, get ready for the job market and connect with employers.
Learn more about career planning
Health & wellness
Health and wellness are essential to student success seeing as university life is both mentally and physically demanding. Concordia offers many professional student services  that put your needs first.
Learn more about Health & wellness services
Social networking
An important part of the university experience is meeting new people, making friends and having fun! Joining a student group or attending a social event  are a couple good ways to break the ice.
Learn more about student groups
