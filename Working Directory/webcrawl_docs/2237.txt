Professor, Simone de Beauvoir Institute & Womens Studies

Chantal Maillé holds a Ph.D. in Political Science from  Université du Québec à Montréal. She joined the faculty of Concordia University  in 1989 where she is Professor of Women’s Studies. She served as Principal of  the Simone de Beauvoir Institute between 1995 and 1999 and between 2008 and  2009. Dr Maillé conducts research in the field of francophonie, feminisms, postcolonial theories and intersectionality. Her most recent co-edited books are Le sujet du féminisme est-il blanc? Femmes racisées et recherche féministe, (Éditions du remue ménage, 2015) and Revealing Democracy:  Secularism and Religion in Liberal Democratic States (Peter  Lang, 2014).  Her recent  publications have addressed issues of transnationalism, feminisms,  postcolonial theories and intersectionality.  She is the author of Les Québécoises et la  conquête du pouvoir politique (Montréal, St-Martin, 1990), Primed for  Power (Canadian Advisory Council on the Status of Women, Ottawa, 1990), Cherchez  la femme. Trente ans de débats constitutionnels au Québec (Montréal, éditions du remue-ménage, 2002), and co-editor with feminist political  scientists Diane Lamoureux and Micheline de Sève of Malaises identitaires échanges féministes autour d’un Québec incertain, (Montréal, éditions du  remue-ménage, 1999). In collaboration with Nancy Guberman and Pierre Maheu she  wrote two books on caregivers and social policies: Et si l'amour ne  suffisait pas (1992) and Travail et soins aux proches dépendants (1993). She has published articles in Women: A Cultural Review, Bulletin  d'histoire politique, Québec Studies, Globe, Revue internationale  d’édudes québécoises, Journal of Canadian Studies, Politique et Sociétés,  Cahiers du CEDREF, Recherches féministes, Zeitschrift für Kanada-Studien,  Estudios Feministas, The Gerontologist, Journal of the Gerontological Society  of America. Chantal Maillé is also a member of RéQEF, Réseau québécois en études féministes (http://reqef.uqam.ca/), and a collaborator of  CRIDAQ, le Centre de recherche interdisciplinaire  sur la diversité (http://www.cridaq.uqam.ca/) , a research center based at UQAM  that studies plurinationalisms in Quebec and in Canada. She serves on the  editorial committee of Recherches féministes.
