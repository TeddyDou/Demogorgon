Office of the Provost
& VP, Academic Affairs
The Office of the Provost & VP, Academic Affairs manages all academic matters, including curriculum appraisals, contract negotiations for faculty members, and academic facilities. The Provost’s team also oversees enrolment and student services, promotes academic leadership and supports faculty to help students succeed.

The Office of the Provost is comprised of the following units:
Student Services
Student Services oversee and coordinate the services students need and expect, offering health care and counseling services, as well as managing recreation, athletics and on-campus residences, among others.
Deputy Provost:  Lisa Ostiguy

Andrew Woodall, Dean of Students

Faculty Relations
The central mission of the university is facilitated by a work environment that accords with applicable collective agreements, provides necessary training and support, and handles conflicts in a fair, collaborative and transparent manner. One of the central missions of the Faculty Relations group is to help develop such a work environment for all academic staff.
Vice-Provost, Faculty Relations:  Nadia Hardy

Innovation in Teaching and Learning
Teaching and Learning assists faculty members with curriculum development and program appraisals, and offers teaching resources in an effort to create a learning environment that drives students to excel at university and in society.
Vice-Provost, Innovation in Teaching and Learning: Sandra Gabriele

Partnerships and Experiential Learning
The Vice-Provost, Partnerships and Experiential Learning will champion and grow partnerships with external organizations (industry, community, not-for-profit) to expand opportunities for experiential learning, cooperative education, extra- and co-curricular activities, and academic community engagement.
Vice-Provost, Partnerships and Experiential Learning:  Nadia Bhuiyan
Reporting to the VP, Partnerships and Experiential Learning:
Claude Martel, Director, Institute for Co-Operative Education
Planning and Positioning
The Vice-Provost, Planning and Positioning will oversee the development of robust institutional planning capacity, including an integrated enrolment management plan, and an academic recruitment strategy that advances research and program objectives.
Vice-Provost, Planning and Positioning:  Anne Whitelaw
Reporting to the VP, Planning and Positioning:
Daniel Therrien, Registrar
Matt Stiegmeyer, Director of Recruitment

Concordia Continuing Education
Concordia Continuing Education (CCE) delivers a wide range of innovative, non-credit courses and programs. CCE, one of Quebec’s finest continuing education providers, is committed to offering local and international students the opportunity to pursue lifelong learning to fulfil personal, professional or academic goals.
Director, Concordia Continuing Education: Isabel Dunnigan
